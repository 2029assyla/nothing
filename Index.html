Emma Watson, the British actress, gained widespread fame for her portrayal of Hermione Granger in the "Harry Potter" film series. Here are the eight "Harry Potter" films in which she starred in and other movies she has been in: "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (released as "Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone" in the US) - 2001, "Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets" - 2002,"Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban" - 2004, "Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire" - 2005, "Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix" - 2007, "Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince" - 2009, "Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1" - 2010 "Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2" - 2011 Aside from the "Harry Potter" series, Emma Watson has appeared in several other notable films, including: "The Perks of Being a Wallflower" (2012), "The Bling Ring" (2013), "Noah" (2014) "Beauty and the Beast" (2017). These films, along with the "Harry Potter" series, are among Emma Watson's most famous works in the world of cinema.

Emma Watson was born on April 15, 1990, in Paris, France. She grew up primarily in Oxfordshire, England, after moving there with her family at a young age. Watson attended the Dragon School, a preparatory school in Oxford, before moving on to Headington School, also in Oxford. Later, she studied at Brown University, graduating with a bachelor's degree in English literature in 2014.

Emma Watson began appearing in movies with her debut as Hermione Granger in "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (released as "Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone" in the US) in 2001. This marked the start of her acting career, and she continued to portray Hermione Granger throughout the entire "Harry Potter" film series, which concluded with "Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2" in 2011..
